# Math Questions

Three written problems, from linear algebra to multivariable calculus. Work through them in order and stop
when the material becomes unfamiliar — leaving a problem blank is expected and tells us where to start you.

Fill in your answers in the provided `math/solutions.md` (it has a section per problem), **or** delete a
section and commit a clear photo/scan under `math/` instead. **Show your working**, not just the final answer.

---

## M-1 — Linear Algebra

Let

$$A=\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\1&2\end{pmatrix}.$$

Compute the following, showing your work:

**(a)** $\det(A)$.

**(b)** The inverse $A^{-1}$.

**(c)** The eigenvalues of $A$.

**(d)** In **one sentence**, describe the geometric meaning of the eigenvector corresponding to the
**larger** eigenvalue (think in terms of what $A$ does to that direction).

---

## M-2 — Single-Variable Calculus

Let $f(x) = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x$.

**(a)** Compute $f'(x)$.

**(b)** Find all critical points of $f$ and classify each as a local maximum or local minimum (state how
you decided).

**(c)** Evaluate the definite integral

$$\int_0^3 f(x)\,dx.$$

---

## M-3 — Multivariable & Matrix Calculus

Let $f(x, y) = x^2 y + \sin(xy)$.

**(a)** Compute both partial derivatives, $\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x}$ and $\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial y}$.

**(b)** Write the gradient vector $\nabla f$.

**(c)** Let $z = Wx$, where $W$ is an $m \times n$ matrix and $x \in \mathbb{R}^n$. State the Jacobian
$\dfrac{\partial z}{\partial x}$ and give its shape.
